Nishat Bagh, Srinagar

Nishat Bagh, or the "Garden of Bliss," is a historic 17th-century Mughal garden in Srinagar, Kashmir, designed in 1633 by Asif Khan, brother of Nur Jehan. Spanning 46 acres, it is famous for its 12 terraced levels representing the zodiac signs, a central water channel with fountains, and stunning views of Dal Lake against the Zabarwan Mountains. It serves as a premier destination for history, nature, and photography enthusiasts, offering a serene escape with its lush greenery and vibrant floral displays.

Kerala

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," offers diverse experiences ranging from serene backwaters to misty hill stations. Top destinations include Munnar for its sprawling tea plantations, Alleppey (Alappuzha) for iconic houseboat cruises, and Varkala for its unique cliffside beaches. To experience Kerala's culture and nature, visit the capital, Thiruvananthapuram, the cultural hub Thrissur, and the wildlife-rich Wayanad or Periyar National Park.
